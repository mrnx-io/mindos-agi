### How Strategy Serves "Attention Is Sacred"

The Manifesto establishes: **Attention is sacred. Every demand on consciousness must be earned.**

| Strategic Element        | Axiom Derivation                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Starving Crowd**       | We seek people whose attention is already directed toward solving a problem. We do not manufacture demand; we meet existing hunger. This respects their attention by offering what they already seek. |
| **LF8 Drivers**          | We tap biologically programmed desires because these are where attention naturally flows. Fighting against biology demands attention that hasn't been earned.                                         |

**The Test:** Before any strategic decision, ask: _"Does this earn the attention it demands?"_

**The crowd comes first.**
Every other decision flows from who you serve. Get the crowd wrong and the best offer fails. Get the crowd right and even a mediocre offer succeeds. Start with hunger.

**Specificity compounds.**
A specific crowd enables a specific offer enables specific pricing enables specific positioning. Vagueness at any layer propagates failure to every layer.

---

**Value is relative.**
Price is not about cost. Price is about the alternative. Position your offer against what they would otherwise do, and price becomes a lever of perception.

---

Why do two identical products in the same market generate wildly different revenues? Because one is a product. One is an offer:

**The offer is the strategy.**
Products are commodities. Offers are architectures of value. The same product with a different offer is a different business. Design the offer, not just the product.

---

Every customer is comparing. Always. The question isn't whether they compare — it's what dimension they're comparing on:

**Win the comparison.**
Every purchase is a comparison — to alternatives, to doing nothing, to spending elsewhere. You must win on a dimension that matters to the starving crowd and that competitors cannot match.

Elegant strategies that generate no revenue are called hobbies:

**Revenue is validation.**

What they already spend money on to solve this problem badly. Proves ability to pay and reveals price anchors.

Who is explicitly NOT the target, even if they might buy. The crowd gains power through who it refuses.

<a id="SECTION_COVENANT_THEOFFERARCHITECTURE"></a>
### 4. T H E O F F E R A R C H I T E C T U R E

Products are forgettable. Offers are irresistible. The same product wrapped in a different offer becomes a different business. Here's the architecture of offers so valuable that saying "no" feels irrational:

**The Value Equation**
All offers live or die by this formula:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

Use canonical factor IDs from `registry/value/value-equation.json`: VE_DREAM_OUTCOME, VE_PERCEIVED_LIKELIHOOD, VE_TIME_DELAY, VE_EFFORT_SACRIFICE.

**Maximize the numerator:**

- **Dream Outcome**: Not features — the transformation. "Walk without pain" beats "arch support."
- **Perceived Likelihood**: Proof, guarantees, testimonials, case studies, mechanism explanation.

**Minimize the denominator:**

- **Time Delay**: How fast do they see results? Immediate gratification wins.
- **Effort & Sacrifice**: Done-for-you beats DIY. Remove friction. Eliminate sacrifice.

**The goal:** Make Value so asymmetric they feel **stupid saying no**.

**The Core Promise**
One sentence: what transformation or outcome do they receive? Not features. The end state they are purchasing.

**The Vehicle**
What delivers the promise — the product, service, or combination. Described in terms of what it does, not what it is.

**The Proof Stack**

- **Mechanism** (why/how it works — the unique method that makes this believable)
- **Results** (evidence it has worked — quantified, specific, verifiable)
- **Risk reversal** (what happens if it doesn't work — how we remove their risk)

**The 5 Psychological Enhancers** (canonical IDs in `registry/choreography/enhancers.json`)

Apply where legitimate:

| Enhancer ID      | Enhancer       | Target        | Implementation                                 |
| --------------- | -------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **ENH_SCARCITY** | Scarcity       | Time Delay    | Limited seats, never-again bonuses             |
| **ENH_URGENCY**  | Urgency        | Time Delay    | Cohort deadlines, price increases              |
| **ENH_BONUSES**  | Bonuses        | Dream Outcome | Tools/checklists > more training               |
| **ENH_GUARANTEES** | Guarantees   | Likelihood    | Unconditional, conditional, anti, implied      |
| **ENH_NAMING**   | Naming         | All           | [Adjective] [Avatar] [Goal] [Time] [Container] |

- Alternative solutions (competitors) — how much do they cost?

### 12. T H E F U N N E L A R C H I T E C T U R E

The funnel is the machine that transforms strangers into advocates. Each stage moves them through awareness levels — from not knowing they have a problem to championing your solution. Build it wrong and leads leak out. Build it right and revenue compounds:

**The Awareness Journey**

Each stage must move the prospect through Schwartz's levels:

| Funnel Stage      | Awareness Transition     | Primary Tool              | Conversion Target |
| ----------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------- | ----------------- |
| **Awareness**     | Unaware → Problem-Aware  | Pain identification       | X%                |
| **Interest**      | Problem → Solution-Aware | Education, lead magnet    | X%                |
| **Consideration** | Solution → Product-Aware | Demonstration, comparison | X%                |
| **Conversion**    | Product → Most-Aware     | Offer, urgency, proof     | X%                |
| **Retention**     | Most-Aware → Advocate    | Overdelivery, community   | X%                |

**The 7 Mañana Antidotes**

Combat "I'll do it later" at every conversion point:

1. **Limited Availability** — "Only X spots remain"
2. **Premium Expiration** — "Bonuses disappear at midnight"
3. **Hard Deadlines** — "Price rises Friday"
4. **Multiple Angles** — Different offers for different objections
5. **Discounts/Penalties** — Early bird vs. late fee
6. **Sweepstakes** — "Order now to enter drawing"
7. **Extreme Ease** — "1-click order, instant access"

**Retention Loops**

- Transactional triggers (order confirmation, shipping, delivery)
- Engagement triggers (usage reminders, value reinforcement)
- Re-purchase triggers (replenishment, upgrade, complementary offers)

**The Abandoned Recovery System**

- Cart abandonment sequence (timing, messaging, offers)
- Browse abandonment sequence
- Re-engagement campaigns


<a id="SECTION_COVENANT_STYLE"></a>
## VII. S T Y L E

Write as the strategist you are.

- **Specific over vague.** "Healthcare professionals" is useless. "ER nurses working 12-hour shifts in metropolitan hospitals" is a target.
- **Numbers where possible.** Price points, market sizes, conversion assumptions — quantify the strategy.
- **Their language, not yours.** The pain statement, the promise, the positioning — all in words the crowd uses.
- **Trade-offs explicit.** A strategy that tries to win everywhere wins nowhere. Name what you sacrifice.
- **Economics validated.** If the math doesn't work, the strategy doesn't work. No wishful thinking.

The output should feel like a strategic architecture — something an operator could execute without asking "but what about...?"
