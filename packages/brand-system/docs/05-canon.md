<a id="SECTION_CANON_CONSTRAINTS"></a>
## III. C O N S T R A I N T S

Rules without constraints become suggestions. Suggestions drift. Drift kills brands. Here's what keeps the grammar honest:

**The Slide comes first.**
Every rule must serve the Slippery Slide — creating flow without resistance. Rules that create friction in reading fail the axiom.

**Rules, not examples.**
Examples illustrate; rules govern. Every section must contain constraints that can be applied to situations not yet imagined.

**Generative, not exhaustive.**
The canon should enable a designer or writer to produce new work that is recognizably on-brand without having seen it before. If it only covers what exists, it will fail when something new is needed.

**Awareness determines approach.**
Voice adapts to Schwartz's five awareness levels. The same message requires different lead archetypes depending on where the reader is in their journey.

**Degrees of freedom are explicit.**
What may vary without identity loss? Name it. The canon must distinguish between invariants (never change) and variables (change within bounds).

**Tokens over adjectives.**
Where possible, express constraints as tokens (spacing values, type scales, color codes, timing) rather than vibes ("clean," "modern," "premium"). Vibes drift. Tokens hold.

**Crowd language is sacred.**
The Lexicon and Voice Rules must use the Starving Crowd's language (from the Covenant), not marketing jargon.

**Conflicts defer to the Ladder.**
When a voice rule conflicts with a form invariant, the Tradeoff Ladder determines which wins.

**The Paradox governs all.**
Every expression must satisfy both tests: a rationalist finds nothing to criticize AND a poet finds something they cannot name.

_The constraints are set. Now: the required atoms._

#### Key Terms + Definitions

Words central to the brand, precisely defined. These are the atoms of brand language.

<a id="SECTION_CANON_SACRED_WORDS"></a>
#### Sacred Words

Terms that carry unusual weight — often from the Manifesto. These words should appear rarely, earning their entry each time.

**Sacred Word Characteristics:**

- They trace directly to the Axiom or a Law
- They cannot be substituted without loss
- They create resonance when the reader encounters them
- They accumulate meaning across touchpoints

**Examples from the philosophy:**

| Sacred Word   | Source      | Weight                               |
| ------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Attention** | Axiom       | The finite resource we honor         |
| **Earned**    | Law 1       | The cost everything must pay         |
| **Crossing**  | Law 5       | The moment of transformation         |
| **Ma**        | Sumi Breath | The interval that creates meaning    |
| **Heartbeat** | Vision      | What we optimize for (vs. algorithm) |
| **Entropy**   | Condition   | The sickness we combat               |

<a id="SECTION_CANON_FORBIDDEN_WORDS"></a>
#### Forbidden Words

Terms that are off-brand, even if common in the category. These words violate the philosophy.

**Forbidden Word Criteria:**

- They optimize for algorithm over heartbeat
- They demand attention without earning it
- They create urgency that feels manufactured
- They speak to reflexes, not consciousness

**Common Forbidden Patterns:**

| Category             | Forbidden                           | Why                                        |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Urgency Hype         | "ACT NOW!" "LIMITED TIME!" "HURRY!" | Demands without earning                    |
| Empty Superlatives   | "Revolutionary" "Game-changing"     | Claims without substance                   |
| Manipulation Tells   | "Secrets they don't want you to..." | Creates false scarcity of information      |
| Discount Language    | "Cheap" "Budget" "Affordable"       | Degrades value perception                  |
| Complexity Signaling | "Synergy" "Leverage" "Optimize"     | Jargon that obscures rather than clarifies |

<a id="SECTION_CANON_CROWD_VOCABULARY"></a>
#### Crowd Vocabulary

Terms the Starving Crowd uses — from the Covenant. These are the words they speak to themselves when describing their pain and desire.

**Crowd Language Principles:**

- Their words, not ours
- Pain language > aspirational language for entry
- Specific > abstract (they don't say "discomfort," they say what hurts)
- Respect their sophistication level

### 2. T H E S I X V O I C E M O D E S

Voice adapts to awareness level. The same philosophy, the same brand — but different approaches based on where the reader is in their journey.

**The Voice Mode Matrix:**

| Mode                  | Approach        | Awareness Fit          | Directness    |
| --------------------- | --------------- | ---------------------- | ------------- |
| **Offer Mode**        | Direct          | Most Aware             | Direct        |
| **Promise Mode**      | Direct          | Product/Solution Aware | Direct        |
| **Bridge Mode**       | Moderate        | Solution/Problem Aware | Moderate      |
| **Secret Mode**       | Indirect        | Problem/Unaware        | Indirect      |
| **Proclamation Mode** | Strong Indirect | Unaware                | High Indirect |
| **Story Mode**        | High Indirect   | Skeptical/Unaware      | Narrative     |

**Cross-Reference Mapping: Voice Modes → Visual System**

| Voice Mode        | Sumi Breath Substance     | Dominant Ma Level             | Energy Posture        |
| ----------------- | ------------------------- | ----------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Offer Mode        | Sumi (commitment)         | Minimal — direct path         | High certainty        |
| Promise Mode      | Kakemono (descent)        | Moderate — breathing room     | Building anticipation |
| Bridge Mode       | Grain (evidence)          | Comfortable — proof space     | Logical flow          |
| Secret Mode       | Yūgen (shadow)            | Generous — mystery breathes   | Curiosity pull        |
| Proclamation Mode | Ma (interval)             | Maximum — statement needs air | Proclamation weight   |
| Story Mode        | Living Tension (boundary) | Narrative rhythm              | Emotional arc         |

_Use this mapping when designing visual containers for copy. Voice mode determines the spatial and energetic treatment._

---

<a id="SECTION_CANON_MODE_1_OFFERMODE_DIRECT"></a>
#### Mode 1: O F F E R M O D E (Direct)

**Best for:** Most Aware prospects ready to buy

**Characteristics:**

- Opens with compelling detail
- States value benefit immediately
- Elaborates on specifics
- Gives reason why (justification)

**Syntax Rules:**

- Lead with the offer itself
- Price justification follows naturally
- CTA appears early and often
- Urgency is real, not manufactured

**Voice Posture:** Peer to peer. "You know what this is. Here's why now."

**Example Pattern:**

> "[Product] at [Price] — [Time Constraint]. [Reason Why.]"

**Brand Adaptation:** Even in direct mode, the Five Laws apply. Urgency must be earned, not demanded. Space must breathe. The threshold must be honored.

---

<a id="SECTION_CANON_MODE_2_PROMISEMODE_DIRECT"></a>
#### Mode 2: P R O M I S E M O D E (Direct)

**Best for:** Product/Solution Aware prospects evaluating options

**Characteristics:**

- Opens with the USP (Unique Selling Proposition)
- Specific, unique, mass-appeal promise
- Mechanism explanation
- Proof before ask

**Syntax Rules:**

- Lead with the promised result
- Differentiate through mechanism ("how" not just "what")
- Social proof reinforces promise
- CTA tied to promise fulfillment

**Voice Posture:** Expert guide. "Here's exactly what you'll get."

**USP Formula (Reeves):**

- **Specific:** Exact benefit, not vague claim
- **Unique:** Only we offer it this way
- **Mass-appeal:** Addresses wide desire

**Brand Adaptation:** The promise must be a promise about relief (`LF8_PAIN_FREEDOM`), ease (`LF8_COMFORT`), or edge (`LF8_SUPERIORITY`) — aligned to brand's LF8 hierarchy.

---

<a id="SECTION_CANON_MODE_3_BRIDGEMODE_MODERATE_INDIRECT"></a>
#### Mode 3: B R I D G E M O D E (Moderate Indirect)

**Best for:** Solution/Problem Aware prospects feeling pain

**Characteristics:**

- Opens with problem recognition
- Builds empathy before solution
- Presents product as natural answer
- Proof addresses specific doubts

**Sub-Patterns:**

| Pattern         | Opening                         | Best For                        |
| --------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **If-Then**     | "If you struggle with X..."     | Clear problem, unclear solution |
| **Inversion**   | "What if X wasn't the problem?" | Reframing opportunity           |
| **ID**          | "Are you a [type] who..."       | Identity-based segmentation     |
| **Question**    | "Do you ever feel..."           | Emotional entry                 |
| **Instruction** | "Stop doing X. Start Y."        | Behavior change                 |

**Voice Posture:** Concerned ally. "I see what you're going through."

**Brand Adaptation:** The Damaging Admission (Kennedy) works powerfully here — acknowledge where we don't excel to build credibility for where we do.

---

<a id="SECTION_CANON_MODE_4_SECRETMODE_INDIRECT"></a>
#### Mode 4: S E C R E T M O D E (Indirect)

**Best for:** Problem/Unaware prospects needing curiosity hook

**Characteristics:**

- Opens with intriguing benefit
- Introduces via headline tease
- Never fully discloses the "secret"
- Escalates clues throughout

**Syntax Rules:**

- Create curiosity gap
- Hints, not reveals
- Build desire to know
- Resolution comes only through action

**Techniques:**

| Technique              | Method                             | Example                                           |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Neologizing**        | Invent new term for known concept  | "Neural Calibration" instead of "adjustment"      |
| **Transubstantiation** | Rename commodity as discovery      | "Foundation System" instead of "[commodity noun]" |
| **Velvet Pouch**       | Tease benefits without full reveal | "The method ancient craftsmen used..."            |

**Voice Posture:** Wise holder of knowledge. "There's something you should know."

**Brand Adaptation:** Mystery (Yūgen) is native to the brand. Secret Mode aligns perfectly with Law 2 (Pregnant Void) — what is omitted does not disappear; it becomes potential.

---

<a id="SECTION_CANON_MODE_5_PROCLAMATIONMODE_STRONG_INDIRECT"></a>
#### Mode 5: P R O C L A M A T I O N M O D E (Strong Indirect)

**Best for:** Unaware prospects needing shock entry

**Characteristics:**

- Opens with bold, shocking statement
- Implies strong promise
- Keeps relevance to offer
- Closes with return to claim

**Syntax Rules:**

- Statement must be true (no clickbait)
- Shock serves education, not manipulation
- Promise embedded in proclamation
- Resolution requires engagement

**Voice Posture:** Herald of truth. "The world has changed. Pay attention."

**Example Pattern:**

> "[Bold Statement]: [Statistic or Evidence]. [Implication for Reader]."

**Brand Adaptation:** Proclamations must honor Sacred Friction (Law 4). The shock should ground, not destabilize. It should wake up, not alarm.

---

<a id="SECTION_CANON_MODE_6_STORYMODE_HIGH_INDIRECT"></a>
#### Mode 6: S T O R Y M O D E (High Indirect)

**Best for:** Skeptical/Unaware prospects with resistance

**Characteristics:**

- Narrative immersion
- Golden thread (emotion tie throughout)
- Unresolved hook
- TPM (Transformational Philosophical Message)

**Story Elements:**

| Element            | Function              | Requirement           |
| ------------------ | --------------------- | --------------------- |
| **Opening Scene**  | Establishes world     | Sensory, immediate    |
| **Tension**        | Creates forward pull  | Unresolved question   |
| **Transformation** | Shows change possible | Before/After embedded |
| **Message**        | Philosophic takeaway  | Connects to Axiom     |

**Voice Posture:** Storyteller. "Let me tell you about someone like you."

**Brand Adaptation:** Story Mode IS the brand's natural home. The entire philosophy emerges from narrative (Ando, Hara, the garden). Stories told must honor the Five Laws within their telling.

- Does it serve the appropriate LF8 driver?
- Does it match the awareness level?

- Does it pass the Paradox test (logic + mystery)?

**Brand Adaptation Rules:**

- All headlines must honor the Five Laws
- Forbidden words never appear in headlines
- Sacred words appear only when weight is justified
- Headlines for Most Aware can be shorter; Unaware require more

<a id="SECTION_CANON_THEVOICERULES"></a>
### 6. T H E V O I C E R U L E S

<a id="SECTION_CANON_SYNTAX"></a>
#### Syntax

**Sentence Length Protocol:**

- First sentences: Short. Punchy. Entry.
- Build to longer sentences that let thought breathe and expand.
- Return to short for emphasis.
- Average: 15-20 words (Flesch 60-70 target)

**Paragraph Protocol:**

- 1-3 sentences maximum
- One idea per paragraph
- White space is copy (Ma principle)

**Structural Rhythms:**

- Vary sentence length deliberately
- Short. Medium. Then a longer sentence that lets the thought breathe and expand into its full meaning. Punch.
- This creates music in prose

<a id="SECTION_CANON_PACE"></a>
#### Pace

**Pacing Modes:**

| Context     | Pace     | Characteristics             |
| ----------- | -------- | --------------------------- |
| Headlines   | Staccato | Punchy, immediate, no waste |
| Body Copy   | Flowing  | Builds momentum, slides     |
| Transitions | Breath   | Ma moments, threshold honor |
| CTAs        | Direct   | Clear, singular, earned     |

**The Breath Before Motion:**
Before every CTA, create a moment of stillness. The 80ms bow in prose form — a short sentence, a pause, then the invitation.

Apply the Sumi Breath motion tokens to copy rhythm:

- **Bow:** pre-CTA micro-pause line break
- **Whisper:** micro-affirmation sentence
- **Breath:** consequence sentence
- **Room:** short whitespace block before the ask

<a id="SECTION_CANON_POSTURE"></a>
#### Posture

**Primary Posture:** Concerned Expert

The voice knows more than the reader but serves them completely. Not above. Not below. Alongside, slightly ahead, holding a light.

**Posture Characteristics:**

- Never condescending
- Never desperate
- Never false-humble
- Always confident without arrogance
- Concern for reader > concern for sale

**The God Complex Applied:**

- **Conviction:** Voice never apologizes for itself
- **Concern:** Voice always serves reader's need
- **Focus:** Voice never chases approval

<a id="SECTION_CANON_TABOO_MOVES"></a>
#### Taboo Moves

**Never:**

- Use exclamation points in body copy (Headlines sparingly)
- Create false urgency ("Last chance!" unless truly last)
- Manipulate through fear without offering relief
- Use jargon the Starving Crowd doesn't use
- Promise without ability to deliver
- Claim superiority directly (let the work prove it)
- Use forbidden words from the Lexicon

**The Damaging Admission (Kennedy):**

When appropriate, acknowledge where we don't excel to build credibility:

> _"We're not the cheapest. We're not the fastest. What we are is [genuine differentiator]."_

Formula: Admit weakness → Pivot to strength → Turn liability into asset

_Voice rules create consistency. But what about form?_

<a id="SECTION_CANON_BOUNDARY_DECISION_PROTOCOL"></a>
#### Boundary Decision Protocol

When uncertain whether you're within degrees of freedom:

1. Does it serve the LF8 hierarchy? (If no, stop)
2. Does it honor the Five Laws? (If no, stop)
3. Does it pass the Paradox test? (If no, revise)
4. Does it maintain Slippery Slide? (If no, revise)
5. Does it use only crowd vocabulary? (If no, revise)

If all five pass, proceed. If any fail, defer to the Tradeoff Ladder.

## V. S T Y L E

Write as the grammarian you are.

- **Tokens over prose** where specificity serves. Include actual values from Sumi Breath.
- **Rules are imperative.** "Use X" not "We tend to use X."
- **Examples support rules, not replace them.** Show one example per rule, then move on.
- **Cross-reference source layers.** When a rule derives from a specific law or token, cite it.
- **Slippery Slide within the canon itself.** This document should be irresistible to read.

The output should feel like a language specification — something a new practitioner could study and then produce work indistinguishable from the founder's intent.

> _"The canon is not a constraint. The canon is freedom —_
> _freedom from endless debate, freedom from inconsistency,_
> _freedom from the slow death of a brand that cannot hold its shape."_
